## Misconceptions

Myth 1: Valuation is an objective search of truth

	• Truth 1.1: All valuations are biased
	• Truth 1.2: The direction of the valuation is proportional to who pays and how much

Myth 2: A good valuation provides a precise estimate of value

	• Truth 2.1:  There are no precise valuations
	• Truth 2.2: The payment of the valuation is greatest when the valuation is least precise

Myth 3: The more quantitative the model, the better the valuation

	• Truth 3.1: The more inputs are in the model the more difficult to understand
	• Truth 3.2: Simpler models do better than complex ones.

You have to manage

	• Bias
	• Uncertainty
	• Complexity

Approaches to Valuation

	• Intrinsic Valuation
	• Relative Valuation or Pricing (comparing)
	• Contingent Claim Valuation or Option
